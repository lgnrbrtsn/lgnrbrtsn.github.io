Current series: Jesus and the Disinherited by Howard Thurman

Meeting Monday evenings starting 7pm MTN, March 29 through April 26

Howard Thurman was one who made the paths straight for the African American Civil Rights Movement of the 1960s. A forerunner and patriarch to those who would organize, struggle, and demand justice out of the legacy of slavery and the American Jim Crow South, Thurman offers an interpretation of the teachings of Jesus through the experience of the oppressed. Through moral analysis grounded in "the religion of Jesus," Thurman commands a vision for emancipation for any who seek righteousness in the face of the multiple dehumanizing systems that corrupt and control our lives; chiefly: racism and white supremacy.

Meeting Tuesday evenings starting 7pm MTN, October 10th through November 7th on Around (it's like Zoom). Sign up here.

Minister and advocate Kevin Nye introduces readers to the Christ he's met in tents, shelters, and drop-in centers. In an approachable way, Nye answers the basic questions and provides a bigger framework toward how people of faith can engage homelessness in their local community. Through a Christian lens, Nye presents the various best practices and challenges to ending homelessness, including housing first, structural racism, economic justice, advocacy and many other approaches and considerations.

Centered in and around Denver, Colorado, we'll use Nye's work as a lens to view Mayor Mike Johnston's new Homeless Initiative which seeks to house 1,000 unsheltered individuals and permanently close encampments by the end of 2023. Where does the plan succeed and where could it improve? As Christians, how can we partner with and encourage civic leadership to ensure everyone in our community is treated as Christ would treat them?
